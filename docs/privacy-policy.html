Privacy Policy

Household Reset · Last updated: April 27, 2026

Household Reset (“the app”) is provided by the Household Reset team. This privacy policy explains what data the app collects, how it is used, and the choices you have.

1. Data we collect

  • Account data: email address and a display name, provided when you sign in.
  • Household data: the household name, invite code, member list, staple items, recurring tasks, shopping list items, and meal suggestions you create.
  • Credit ledger: a record of credit balances and grants (initial signup, rewarded ads watched, referrals redeemed) used to operate the credit system that powers AI meal suggestions.
  • Referral codes: each account has a unique 6-character code visible only to you and used to attribute referral bonuses when shared.
  • Usage events: anonymous analytics events (e.g. task_completed) used to improve the product. These events do not include the content of your items.

2. How we use it

  • To operate the app — sync your household across devices and members, send reminder notifications you opt into, and generate meal suggestions.
  • To diagnose crashes and errors.
  • We do not sell your data, and we do not share it with advertisers beyond the identifiers required by the ad networks listed below.

3. Third parties

  • Supabase (backend): stores your account and household data, plus the credit ledger and referral codes.
  • Google Gemini (Meal Rescue AI): receives the pantry items you mark as available so it can suggest dinners. It does not receive your email, display name, household name, or any identifier that ties suggestions back to you.
  • Google AdMob (rewarded video and banner ads): serves ads using standard mobile ad identifiers. You can reset or opt out of the ad identifier in your device settings. Premium subscribers do not see ads.
  • RevenueCat (subscriptions): processes subscription status.
  • Firebase Crashlytics (Google): receives anonymous crash reports and stack traces so we can diagnose and fix bugs. No names, emails, or household contents are included.

4. Your choices

  • You can sign out at any time from Settings.
  • You can permanently delete your account from Settings → Danger Zone → Delete Account. Deletion removes your profile, credits, referral code, household membership, and any household for which you were the sole member. Households with other members survive with ownership transferred.
  • Notifications are opt-in and can be disabled from Settings → Notifications or from your device settings at any time.

5. Deleting your account

You can permanently delete your Household Reset account at any time, directly from inside the app. No support email or web form is required.

Steps

  1. Open the Household Reset app.
  2. Tap the You tab in the bottom navigation bar.
  3. Scroll to the Danger zone section near the bottom.
  4. Tap Delete Account and confirm.

What is deleted immediately

  • Your profile (display name, email, user ID).
  • Your credit balance, credit ledger, and referral code.
  • Your household membership.
  • Any household for which you were the sole remaining member, including its staples, recurring tasks, shopping list, and meal-suggestion history.

What is retained, and for how long

  • Households that still have other members: the household itself survives, with ownership transferred to the next senior member. Your name and activity are removed from member views immediately.
  • Anonymized crash logs (Firebase Crashlytics): retained up to 90 days for debugging, then auto-purged. They contain no personally identifying information.
  • Anonymized analytics events (e.g. task_completed): retained in aggregated form. They contain no personally identifying information and cannot be traced back to you after deletion.
  • Automated database backups: deleted records roll off Supabase's snapshot system within 7 days under normal conditions.

Deletion is immediate and irreversible.

6. Children

The app is not directed to children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect data from them.

7. Changes

We may update this policy. When we do, we will update the “Last updated” date above and, for material changes, notify you in-app.

8. Contact

Questions or concerns? Reach out through the support channel listed on the app’s store page.
